About Us



The Container port benchmarking is a research initiative by Centre for Maritime Studies (CMS) at the National University of Singapore (NUS). The project is funded by Singapore Maritime Institute (SMI) Singapore. Waterborne Transport Insititute (WTI), China is an industry collaborator to support the study.


Background of the study

Our project revolves around the development of a robust container port index system. To ensure a holistic and multi-stakeholder approach, we are seeking input from experts across various domains, including companies that utilize the ports, port operators, port authorities, freight forwarders, ship liners, and logistic companies. By incorporating diverse perspectives, we aim to create an index system that accurately reflects the needs and priorities of the industry.


Objectives

  • To develop a world class container port benchmarking system.
  • To evolve a healthy competition among ports around the globe.
  • To identify the improvement areas in a container port.

Benefits to the Industry

The primary objective of the project is to design a comprehensive assessment model that enables a comparative evaluation of container ports globally. We analyse the specific characteristics, best practices and contextual factors that contribute to the success of container ports. The evaluation can potentially help identify areas of improvement and formulate strategies to enhance the competitiveness and efficiency of ports.
